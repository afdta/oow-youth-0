Out of work sketches
Apr 27, 2017

Notes on strategy based on initial meetings

Primary goal: Allow local policymakers and practitioners to tailor policies to their specific out of work populations.

Communications hurdles

  1. What does "out of work" mean?
  2. What are the policy options for addressing the out of work groups?
  3. How does this play out in MY jurisdiction?

Need to clarify

  1. What is the most helpful frame here? Do the superclusters translate to policy interventions or is it the age/education matrix?
  2. How important are the details of the individual clusters for a given place? If the policy levers derive from the more coarse set of groups (superclusters or age/education groupings), how necessary are the details of the specific clusters? My sense is that the details of each cluster are useful to validate and/or revise the policy recommendations. But, what would a place do if the details of one or more clusters don't seem to jibe with the superset? Of course, the details are interesting for data/policy wonks and that may be sufficient argument for providing the details. Either way, the details should be a "second layer" to the presentation that can be ignored by the reader.
  3. How do we handle the two primary age group divisions (18-24 and 25-64)?
